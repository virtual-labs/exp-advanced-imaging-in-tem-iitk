<b>Bright Field (BF) Imaging: </b><br>
•	<b>Select the objective lens</b> To focus on the sample.<br>
•	<b>Adjust the condenser lens</b> To ensure the electron beam is converging onto the sample (you can use the condenser aperture for finer control).<br>
•	<b>Set the objective aperture</b> Wide open, allowing the unscattered (direct) electrons to pass through.<br>
•	<b>Focus</b> on the sample.<br>
•	<b>Capture the BF image</b>, Which uses the transmitted, unscattered electrons. These electrons create the image of the sample where contrast is typically based on the sample thickness and atomic number.<br><br>

<b>Switch to Dark Field mode: </b><br>
•	Using the selected area diffraction (SAD) aperture, select a diffraction spot corresponding to a particular crystallographic plane or feature you want to observe in the image.<br>
•	<b>Center the diffraction spot</b> in the diffraction plane by adjusting the selected area aperture.<br>
•	<b>Use a smaller objective aperture</b> to block out the transmitted (zero-order) electrons, and allow only the diffracted electrons (from a specific crystal plane or lattice) to pass through.<br>
